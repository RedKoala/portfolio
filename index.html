Astronaut Training with Augmented Reality

Astronauts require costly training on the ground to complete every task on board, from fixing a solar panel to prepping a meal. I led the design team on an augmented reality and IoT prototype for just-in-time training on these tasks to cut costs. Our findings provide initial human-computer interaction guidelines for augmented reality training tools, as published in HCI International 2017.

Prototyping for Maker Faire

I helped create an exhibit at Maker Faire Bay Area 2017, along with the Cupertinker Space makers. I built an IoT stack that enabled participants to kickoff the exhibit from their smartphone, sending a ping-pong ball through a whimsical series of interactive displays: from a Rube-Golderg machine that made a stuffed monkey dance to a giant Dr. Who-themed pinball machine.
